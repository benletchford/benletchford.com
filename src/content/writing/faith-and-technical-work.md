Technical work rewards impatience in the short term. The quick patch, the
clever shortcut, the abstraction that sounds better than it behaves: all of
them can move a project forward for a while.

Faith pulls in the opposite direction. It asks whether the work is truthful,
whether the hidden parts can bear inspection, and whether the people who depend
on the system are being served rather than managed.

That does not make software sacred. It makes ordinary decisions heavier. Naming
a thing well, refusing a false metric, writing the boring test, leaving a clear
path for the next person: these are small practices of attention.
