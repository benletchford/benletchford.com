A personal site should not behave like a feed unless the person building it
wants to become a feed.

The better metaphor is a public folder. Some pieces are polished, some are
small, and some are mostly markers for future work. The structure matters
because it gives a reader somewhere to return to without asking them to keep up.

That is the quiet advantage of a static site. It does not need to perform
freshness. It can simply remain.
